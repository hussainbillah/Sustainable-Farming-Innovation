Project Vision

Our revolutionary model of integrated, sustainable agriculture in Paikgacha, Khulna transforms rural food and energy systems. Starting on 1 acre of land with plans to expand to 10 acres by 2027, we're building a zero-waste, pesticide-free ecosystem that empowers communities while ensuring food security and generating renewable energy. By combining coral fish farming with Black Soldier Fly larvae-based feed, organic poultry in elevated coops, papaya and brinjal cultivation for organic feed, biogas from poultry waste, and hybrid solar-wind energy systems, we've created a completely circular farming approach. This climate-smart agriculture model serves as both a productive farm and research-ready setup for future agricultural innovation in Bangladesh.

Project Components

Coral Fish Farming

We are cultivating coral fish (Koral) in a 1-acre pond using a sustainable feed model based on Black Soldier Fly (BSF) larvae. This feed offers high protein, promotes rapid growth, and eliminates the need for chemical supplements or antibiotics. The system ensures clean, nutrient-rich aquaculture.

Black Soldier Fly Unit

BSF farming will be set up in net-enclosed structures to prevent uncontrolled spreading. The BSF larvae are fed clean, organic matter—primarily papaya and brinjal—grown pesticide-free around the pond. We aim to produce 1500-1800kg of BSF larvae daily, which will be used 75% as feed for both fish and poultry.

Organic Poultry on Elevated Coop

Over the pond water, we will build elevated platforms (Tong houses) to rear 4,000 chickens. These structures reduce land use, improve hygiene, and allow poultry waste to fall directly into collection pits for biogas production.

Papaya & Brinjal Cultivation

We will grow fast-growing, pesticide-free papaya and brinjal around the pond. These crops are exclusively used as feed input for the BSF larvae, ensuring the circular ecosystem remains clean and safe.

Biogas Energy Unit

Poultry waste will be converted into biogas using digesters. The gas will be used for clean cooking by local households, while the slurry will be applied as organic fertilizer—reducing dependency on chemical inputs.

Solar Power System

Rooftop solar panels will be installed over poultry sheds to generate clean energy. The system is designed for easy expansion as the project grows, with smart energy management that integrates with our other power sources.

Wind Turbine Tower

High-capacity wind turbines (30KW to 50KW) will be installed on elevated towers to harness the coastal wind of Paikgacha, ensuring power surplus to distribute locally, especially during monsoon seasons.
